Core idea - You are building an AI-powered assistant — a real-time, intelligent urban platform designed to help citizens navigate the complexities of city life by delivering hyperlocal, personalized, and actionable insights. In bustling cities like Bengaluru, millions of fragmented data points are generated every minute, ranging from traffic conditions and civic issues to spontaneous events and environmental hazards. These pieces of information are often scattered across various platforms, quickly outdated, and difficult for citizens to keep track of or make sense of. Your platform aims to solve this by cutting through the noise, intelligently synthesizing these data streams, and offering clear, meaningful updates to users when and where they matter most.

The assistant features a conversational AI interface, letting users ask natural language questions like “Where’s the nearest open pharmacy?”, “Which metro lines are delayed today?”, or “Suggest good cafes with Wi-Fi around me.” The AI responds instantly with precise, map-linked answers, offering a seamless and proactive urban companion. Unlike static listings or scattered platforms, this assistant transforms chaotic, multi-source urban data into a single, intelligent, and user-friendly interface for everyday city living.

Another key feature is multimodal citizen reporting. The platform empowers users to actively contribute to the city’s data by submitting geo-tagged photos, videos, or reports of events around them — like waterlogging, fallen trees, traffic jams, or public performances. Using AI-powered image and video analysis, these inputs are automatically categorized, described, and plotted onto a real-time city map. This map interface becomes a living, breathing visualization of the city’s pulse, with intuitive emoji markers representing different types of incidents and events.

In essence, what you’re building is not just a city information app — it’s a personalized, predictive, and socially intelligent agent that delivers a real-time, living pulse of the city, giving people the power to stay informed, connected, and prepared in the urban jungle.

Representing Dayalbagh Educational Institute (DEI), Team Sahayog — comprising Adbhut Satsangi,
Ambuj Kulshrestha, Araz Bhatia, B. Apaar Rao, and Sumir Singh — participated in this landmark
hackathon and worked on the problem statement “Managing City Data Overload.” Modern
metropolitan cities like Bengaluru generate enormous volumes of real-time data, including traffic
updates, power outages, civic complaints, and emergency alerts. However, this information is
dispersed across multiple platforms, making it difficult for citizens to access relevant updates while
city authorities struggle with timely decision-making due to unorganized and noisy data streams. The
core challenge lies not in the availability of information but in the absence of a unified, intelligent
system to filter, structure, and deliver actionable insights efficiently.
To address this issue, Team Sahayog designed and developed “NagarChakshu,” an agentic AIpowered urban intelligence platform that functions as a proactive digital assistant for cities. The
solution enables multimodal real-time data ingestion from diverse sources such as social media
feeds, citizen reports, and GPS-based services and applies AI-driven summarization, classification,
and predictive analytics to provide timely and relevant updates. Key features include automated
incident categorization, forecasting of disruptions through predictive modeling, a dynamic city
sentiment and pulse index, personalized location-based alerts, and an AI-powered chatbot with a
continuous feedback loop for enhanced accuracy. Built entirely using Google technologies such as
Firebase, Vertex AI, Cloud Run, and Google Maps Platform, the solution ensures scalability,
efficiency, and real-time responsiveness.
